ABOUT UBP TAIWAN

UBP Taiwan embodies every aspect of the Group’s values. It sums up our corporate culture – one based on dynamism, energy, instinct, dedication and teamwork.
UBP has delicately cultivated the Asian market by providing dedicated investment solutions. After establishing a multinational set-up in Hong Kong and Singapore, UBP Asset Management Taiwan Ltd. began operations on 6th September 2018, having secured a Securities Investment Consulting Enterprises (SICE) business License, with the ability to serve as a Master Agent, from the Financial Supervisory Commission R.O.C (Taiwan). We are now fully established with our own Master Agent business to service our institutional and intermediary clients in the Taiwan market. We aim to introduce unique products for the Taiwan market with flexible and innovative investment strategies.

UBP SA Introduction

Union Bancaire Privée (UBP) was founded in 1969 by Edgar de Picciotto. Our Head Office is in Geneva, Switzerland and we operate from over twenty locations around the world, UBP draws on the strengths of its entire network to combine global expertise with local know-how.

As an independent Swiss private bank focusing on performance and capital preservation, UBP distinguishes itself with its financial robustness. Thanks to careful risk management and having handled the balance sheet conservatively, UBP has a strong financial base. UBP’s Tier 1 ratio of 25.6%, makes it one of the best-capitalised banks in Europe. In an endorsement of these firm foundations, in January 2019, the rating agency Moody’s assigned UBP a long-term deposit rating of Aa2 with stable outlook.

Investors should read the Prospectus and Investor Information Summary (IIS) before subscription.

Investors are advised not to invest a substantial proportion of their portfolio in the Funds which appeal in high yield bonds’ investments. Given high yield bonds are either rated below investment grade or not rated, and are highly sensitive to the fluctuations of interest rates, the Funds may suffer the loss due to the rise of interest rates, the declines of market liquidity, the default of bond issuers who fail to make capital and interest payments, or the bankruptcy of the bond issuers. The Funds are not advisable for investors who cannot undertake relevant risks.

 Pursuant to the FSC's Regulations Governing Offshore Funds, the Fund shall invest no more than 20% of its net asset value in the securities listed and traded on the stock exchanges in the People Republic of China. Investors should also pay special attention to the particular risks in politics, economics and investment markets in the PRC.

Investment in emerging markets may have greater price volatility and liquidity risk than investment in developed countries, and the political and economic stability in emerging markets may be lower than developed countries, hence the value of assets may be affected to varying degrees. Investors shall fully understand the risks and characteristics of such funds before investment.
